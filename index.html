Sample Lesson

ESOL Preview — Everyday English

Lesson Structure

🔤 Warm-Up (5 min)

Review vocabulary from the previous class and quick pronunciation drills to get your mouth moving.

📚 New Topic (20 min)

Focus on one practical grammar point or vocabulary theme — shopping, travel, workplace, daily life.

💬 Conversation Practice (20 min)

Real dialogue practice with role-play scenarios. No judgment, only encouragement.

✏️ Wrap-Up & Homework (5 min)

Quick summary and a short, fun take-home exercise to reinforce what you learned.

🌟 Today's Phrases

  • "Could you say that again, please?"
    Politely ask for repetition
  • "I'm not sure I follow you."
    Express that you don't understand
  • "What do you mean by that?"
    Ask for clarification
  • "Let me put it another way."
    Rephrase your idea clearly
  • "That makes sense now!"
    Confirm understanding

Bible Study Notes

Scripture & Study Journal

A glimpse into how we study the Word — handwritten notes, original Greek, highlighted verses, and deep reflections from our sessions together.

🔍

Isaiah 33 : 22

The LORD Is Our King

"For the LORD is our judge, the LORD is our lawgiver, the LORD is our king; it is he who will save us." Three roles of God — Judge, Lawgiver, King — all pointing to one Saviour.

🔍

Isaiah 33 : 5 – 6

A Sure Foundation

"He will be the sure foundation for your times, a rich store of salvation and wisdom and knowledge; the fear of the LORD is the key to this treasure." Fear of the LORD unlocks everything.

🔍

Col 4:6 · 1 Pet 3:9 · Matt 12:36

Words That Bless

"Let your conversation be always full of grace, seasoned with salt" (Col 4:6). Study notes on speaking blessing instead of criticism — and the solemn weight of every careless word (Matt 12:36).

🔍

1 Corinthians 14 : 4

Edify the Church

"Anyone who speaks in a tongue edifies themselves, but the one who prophesies edifies the church." Deep word-study on edify — to instruct, enlighten, and build up spiritually.

🔍

1 John 3 : 13 – 15

Love & Hatred

"We know that we have passed from death to life, because we love each other." Notes comparing NIV, KJV, and AMP translations — with Korean annotations on the depth of God's love command.

🔍

1 John 3 : 14 (AMP) · Greek Study

ABIDE — μένω (men-o)

Original Greek word study: μένω means to remain, stay, continue to exist, to tolerate, to withstand, to sojourn. "He who does not love remains in spiritual death" — unpacking what it truly means to abide.

🔍

2 Timothy 2 : 1 – 5

A Good Soldier

"Endure hardness, as a good soldier of Jesus Christ." Paul trained Timothy to be a minister — a soldier for Christ on the battlefield. We must learn to endure hardness and pay the price to walk in victory over satan.

🔍

Genesis 1 : 26 – 27 · Page 54

Bonding with the Father

"You must bond with your Father if you want to know Him." God created both male and female in His image. Trusting God is absolutely necessary if you want to bond with Him — the only hindrance is willingness.

🔍

Romans 3:23 · 1 Cor 6:20 · 7:23

Doing the First Works

Three steps to bond with the Father: (1) Accept full responsibility that you have sinned. (2) Accept that Jesus took your place on the cross. (3) Accept that He is the functional Lord of your life from this moment on.

🔍

Genesis 1 : 26 – 27 · Page 53

Bonding

"In the image of God created he him; male and female created he them." Bonding can be illustrated by the relationship between a mother and baby. God created both male and female — the traits of both genders were created by God.

🔍

Revelation 2 : 4 – 5

Choose Jesus as Your First Love

"Because thou hast left thy first love… repent, and do the first works." Step 4: Choose Jesus Christ as your first love and maintain that choice. Ask if He has ever truly been your first love — the truth sets you free to repent.

🔍

Word Study · Latin Root

MAINTAIN — Word Study

Latin root: "hold in the hand." (1) Cause or enable to continue — keep going, keep up, carry on, sustain, bear. (2) Provide with necessities for life or existence. Korean: 지속하다 · 계속하다 · 유지하다 · 지지하다 · 후원하다.

🔍

Ephesians 3 · The Message

The Secret Plan of God

Paul from prison: "I got the inside story from God himself." The mystery of Christ — outsiders and insiders stand on the same ground before God. Same offer, same help, same promise.

🔍

Ephesians 2 · The Message

He Tore Down the Wall

"He took our sin-dead lives and made us alive in Christ… picked us up and set us down in highest heaven with Jesus, our Messiah." Saving is all His idea, and all His work.

🔍

Ephesians 1–6 · The Message

Who I Am in Christ

The God of Glory: "Long before he laid down earth's foundations, he had us in mind — the focus of his love, to be made whole and holy. Long ago he decided to adopt us into his family through Jesus Christ."

🔍

Ephesians 1 · Word Study

PERIPHERAL

"The church is not peripheral to the world; the world is peripheral to the church." PERIPHERAL: relating to · involving · outer part of the field of vision · blood in systemic circulation.

🔍

Word Study

MEEK — Word Study

Gentle, submissive. Synonyms: patient · long-suffering · forbearing · gentle · quiet · reverent · peaceful · mild · humble · lowly · unambitious · submissive · obedient.

🔍

Ephesians 5:21–28 · The Message

Relationships

"Husbands, go all out in your love for your wives, exactly as Christ did for the church — a love marked by giving, not getting. His words evoke her beauty."

🔍

Romans 12:1–2 · Page 129

Repentance & the Body

"Many are learning, but never come to the knowledge of the truth. If you are a hearer and not a doer of the Word, you are deceiving yourself." Present your body a living sacrifice.

🔍

Ephesians 6:10–24 · The Message

A Fight to the Finish

"God is strong and he wants you strong." Truth, righteousness, peace, faith, salvation — indispensable weapons. "Pray hard and long. Pray for your brothers and sisters. Keep each other's spirits up."

🔍

1 Corinthians 13:13 · The Message

LOVE NEVER Dies

Three things to do: TRUST steadily · HOPE unswervingly · LOVE extravagantly. 17 qualities of love: never gives up, cares for others, never flies off the handle, trusts God always, never looks back. "And the best of the three is LOVE."

🔍

1 Cor 13:13 · Prov 3:4–6 · Heb 6:19 · 1 John 3:14

Trust · Hope · Love — Deep Study

TRUST: "Trust God from the bottom of your heart" (Prov 3:5). HOPE: "an anchor for the soul, firm and secure" (Heb 6:19). LOVE: "we have passed from death unto life, because we love the brethren" (1 John 3:14).

🔍

James 4:12 · NIV & MSG

Only One Lawgiver & Judge

NIV: "There is only one Lawgiver and Judge, the one who is able to save and destroy. But you — who are you to judge your neighbour?" MSG: "God is in charge of deciding human destiny. Who do you think you are to meddle in the destiny of others?"

🔍

James 4:12 · Study Notes

Judge & Destiny — Study Notes

Key words circled: meddle (to interfere in what is not your concern) and destiny (God alone holds the final say over human destiny). "Who are you to judge your neighbour?" — only God saves and destroys.

🔍

C.S. Lewis · Quote

Two Kinds of People

"There are only two kinds of people in the end: those who say to God, 'Thy will be done,' and those to whom God says, in the end, 'Thy will be done.' No soul that seriously and constantly desires joy will ever miss it."
